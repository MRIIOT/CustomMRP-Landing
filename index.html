Finally, MRP That Understands Custom Manufacturing

Product-Centric Architecture

The Problem with Other Systems:

Traditional MRP treats all items in an order as one blob. Generic "tasks" that don't tell you what you're actually making.

The Custom MRP Difference:

Processes belong to specific products. "Cut wood for Wall Flat A" vs. "Cut wood for Wall Flat B" are separate, trackable processes. Each product tracks its own progress independently.

Why It Matters:

Crystal clear what you're working on. No confusion between similar items. Perfect for shops making multiple unique pieces per order.

Product Templates (80% Automation)

The Problem with Other Systems:

You rebuild every job from scratch. 45 minutes of data entry every time. High error rates. Inconsistency.

The Custom MRP Difference:

Templates pre-configure materials, processes, estimated times, and custom fields. Customize only the 20% that's unique per job. Create work orders in minutes.

Why It Matters:

Massive time savings. Consistency across similar work. New employees can create accurate work orders. Focus on the custom part, not the data entry.

Smart Material Picking Workflow

The Problem with Other Systems:

Either they ignore materials completely, or they treat inventory like retail (not manufacturing). No enforcement of picking before work starts.

The Custom MRP Difference:

Formal pick process required before starting work. Partial picking supported (real world: materials trickle in). Allocation tracking prevents double-booking. Project-specific inventory auto-hides when done.

Why It Matters:

Never start work without materials ready. Eliminate duplicate purchases. Know what's allocated where. Handle real-world material flow (not just perfect scenarios).

Where Project Management Meets Production

The Problem with Other Systems:

Project management tools (Monday, Asana) handle timelines but ignore materials, inventory, and shop floor workflows. Traditional MRP handles production but treats projects as afterthoughts with poor milestone tracking.

The Custom MRP Difference:

True integration of both worlds. Group work orders into projects with multiple milestones. Track project-level timelines AND production-level details. Material allocations flow across related work orders. Shop floor sees what they need to do; project managers see overall progress.

Why It Matters:

Custom manufacturers need both. You promise delivery dates to customers (project management) while managing materials and shop floor execution (production management). Custom MRP is purpose-built for this reality.
