What It Means for Your Business:

Pick materials BEFORE starting work (the system enforces it). See what's allocated to which job. Get low-stock alerts for your 16 most critical items. Support partial picking when materials trickle in (pick 5 of 7 sheets today, track the 2 still coming).

Track project-specific inventory separately and auto-hide it when jobs complete. Know exactly what you have, where it is, and what it's for.

So you can: Never start a job without materials ready. Never buy duplicates of stuff you already have. Stop wasting money on emergency supply runs.

Feature 3: Clear Priorities for Your Team

What It Means for Your Business:

Every operator sees their assigned work, prioritized and clear. Work Queue shows exactly what needs to be done next. Real-time progress tracking with timers. Item-level tracking within processes (not just "cutting done" but "Frame pieces cut ✓, Window frame cut ✓, Backing pending").

No more interruptions every 30 minutes asking "what should I work on?"

So you can: Keep your team productive without micromanaging. Know work is happening in the right order. Track accountability down to who completed each specific task.

Feature 4: Product Templates That Save Hours

What It Means for Your Business:

Build templates once for your common products—wall flats, door assemblies, LED light boxes, steel frameworks, tables, stages. Pre-configure materials, processes, and custom fields.

When a new job comes in, select the template, customize the 20% that's unique (dimensions, paint color, special requirements), and you're done. 80% of the data entry happens automatically.

So you can: Create work orders in 5 minutes instead of 45. Ensure consistency across similar jobs. Reduce errors from manual data entry. Get new employees productive faster.

What It Means for Your Business:

Custom MRP's product-centric architecture means processes belong to specific products. "Cut wood for Wall Flat A" is crystal clear, not generic "Cut wood" that could mean anything.

So you can: Never confuse which piece you're working on. Handle variety without creating dozens of separate work orders. See exactly which products are done and which aren't.

Feature 6: Project Management for Multi-Job Orders

What It Means for Your Business:

Group related work orders into projects. Track multiple date milestones (start date, due date, pencils-down date, ship date). See all work orders for a project in one place. Create work orders directly from the project screen.

So you can: Handle complex customer projects with multiple deliverables. Keep everything organized when a single show needs 20 different components. Professional project tracking that customers can appreciate.

Finally, MRP That Understands Custom Manufacturing

Product-Centric Architecture

The Problem with Other Systems:

Traditional MRP treats all items in an order as one blob. Generic "tasks" that don't tell you what you're actually making.

The Custom MRP Difference:

Processes belong to specific products. "Cut wood for Wall Flat A" vs. "Cut wood for Wall Flat B" are separate, trackable processes. Each product tracks its own progress independently.

Why It Matters:

Crystal clear what you're working on. No confusion between similar items. Perfect for shops making multiple unique pieces per order.

Product Templates (80% Automation)

The Problem with Other Systems:

You rebuild every job from scratch. 45 minutes of data entry every time. High error rates. Inconsistency.

The Custom MRP Difference:

Templates pre-configure materials, processes, estimated times, and custom fields. Customize only the 20% that's unique per job. Create work orders in minutes.

Why It Matters:

Massive time savings. Consistency across similar work. New employees can create accurate work orders. Focus on the custom part, not the data entry.

Smart Material Picking Workflow

The Problem with Other Systems:

Either they ignore materials completely, or they treat inventory like retail (not manufacturing). No enforcement of picking before work starts.

The Custom MRP Difference:

Formal pick process required before starting work. Partial picking supported (real world: materials trickle in). Allocation tracking prevents double-booking. Project-specific inventory auto-hides when done.

Why It Matters:

Never start work without materials ready. Eliminate duplicate purchases. Know what's allocated where. Handle real-world material flow (not just perfect scenarios).
